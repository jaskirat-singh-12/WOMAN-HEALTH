Breast Health

Breast cancer is a significant concern for women. Regular breast self-exams and mammograms are important for early detection.

Osteoporosis

Women are more prone to osteoporosis than men. It's a condition that weakens bones and makes them more susceptible to fractures.

Thyroid Disorders

Women are more likely to develop thyroid problems, such as hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism.

Reproductive Health

Women experience unique health issues related to the reproductive system. This can include menstrual disorders, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), endometriosis, and infertility.

Heart Health

Heart disease is the leading cause of death in women. Cardiovascular risk factors like high blood pressure and high cholesterol should be monitored.

Obesity

Obesity can lead to various health issues, including diabetes, heart disease, and joint problems. It can be particularly challenging for women.
