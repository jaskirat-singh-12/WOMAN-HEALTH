Dysmenorrhea:

This is the medical term for painful menstruation, also known as menstrual cramps. It can range from mild to severe and is often caused by uterine contractions

Menorrhagia:

Excessive menstrual bleeding, which can lead to heavy periods that last longer than a week. It may be caused by hormonal imbalances or other underlying conditions.

Endometriosis

A condition where tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside of the uterus. It can cause severe pain, heavy bleeding, and fertility problems

Fibroids

Uterine fibroids are noncancerous growths in the uterus that can cause heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, and other symptoms.

Adenomyosis

A condition where the tissue lining the uterus grows into the muscular wall of the uterus. This can lead to heavy bleeding, severe cramps, and pain during menstruation.

Anovulation

When the ovaries do not release an egg during the menstrual cycle, resulting in irregular or absent periods
