*Morning Sickness*:

Many women experience nausea and vomiting, commonly known as morning sickness, especially during the first trimester.

Gestational Diabetes:

Some women develop high blood sugar levels during pregnancy, known as gestational diabetes, which can have health implications for both the mother and the baby.

Preeclampsia

This is a condition characterized by high blood pressure and damage to organs such as the liver and kidneys. It usually occurs after the 20th week of pregnancy and can be life-threatening if left untreated.

Preterm Labor

Preterm labor is when a woman goes into labor before 37 weeks of pregnancy. It can result in complications for the baby as they may not be fully developed.

Anemia

Pregnancy can lead to a decrease in the number of red blood cells, causing anemia. This can lead to fatigue, weakness, and other health issues.

Heartburn

Hormonal changes can lead to relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter, causing heartburn. It's a common discomfort during pregnancy.
